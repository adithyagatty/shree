SUNETRA 2025

An International Ayurveda Conclave on Abhishyanda- Inflammatory Eye Disorders

  • Dates: February 5th, 6th, and 7th, 2025
  • Venue: Sreedhareeyam Ayurvedic Research and Development Institute, Nelliakkattu Mana, Kizhakombu P.O., Koothattukulam, Kerala, 686662, India
  • Theme: "Management of Abhishyanda (Inflammatory Eye Disorders) in Ayurveda"
Event Overview

Sunetra 2025 is an esteemed international academic conclave spotlighting the rich tradition of Kerala’s Ayurvedic excellence in Nethra Chikitsa (eye care), with a special focus on managing Abhishyanda (inflammatory eye disorders). Organized by the Sreedhareeyam Ayurvedic Research and Development Institute, the event brings together global scholars, practitioners, and researchers to celebrate and explore the time-tested wisdom of Kerala’s eye care therapies alongside innovative Ayurvedic approaches to ocular health.

Sub-Themes
Vyadhi (Pathology)

Focuses on understanding the pathological aspects of Abhishyanda, including its etiopathogenesis and systemic associations.

  • Clinical perspectives on Indriya Vaikalyam (sensory impairment).
  • Impact of Abhishyanda on different Netra Avayavas
  • Classification of common eye conditions within the Abhishyanda spectrum.
  • Abhishyanda as a manifestation of systemic disorders (Upadrava).
Chikitsa (Treatment)

Explores diverse therapeutic approaches for managing Abhishyanda, including general, Dosha-specific, and Avayava (organ)-specific treatments.

  • Importance of Agni (digestive health) and Koshta (gut function) in achieving effective outcomes.
  • Reviews of notable Ayurvedic formulations (Oushadha Yogas) that have proven effective in treating Abhishyanda.
  • Clinical experiences and innovative case studies.
Kriya (Procedures)

Focuses on procedural interventions for Santharpana, Apatharpana, Samana, Sodhana, Prasadana, Ropana, etc.

  • Understanding Netrakriyas on the basis of Netra Avayavas
  • Surgical and parasurgical procedures (Sastra-Anusastra).
  • Criteria for selecting suitable interventions (Arha-Anarhatha).
Conference Objectives
  • To provide an in-depth understanding of Abhishyanda’s management through Ayurvedic principles.
  • To showcase Kerala's traditional contributions to Netra Chikitsa
  • To encourage innovation and research in Ayurvedic ophthalmology.
  • To create a platform for academic and clinical expertise.
  • To honor Dr. NPP Namboothiri’s contributions to Ayurveda.
Event Highlights
  • Keynote Addresses: Presentations by leading Ayurvedic scholars.
  • Panel Discussions: Exploring integrative approaches to eye care.
  • Live Demonstrations: Hands-on sessions in Keraleeya Netra Chikitsa.
  • Cultural Programs: Featuring Kathakali and music concert.
About Dr. NPP Namboothiri Memorial Sasthra Jyoti Puraskaram Prize Money: ₹1,00,000

This prestigious award celebrates excellence in Ayurvedic research and innovation. Open to postgraduate and RAV scholars across India, the competition aims to identify and honor the brightest minds in Ayurvedic ophthalmology.

Competition Structure
  • 8-Minute Video Presentations: Participants submit concise videos showcasing research, theoretical or clinical insights related to Ayurvedic Netra Chikitsa .
  • Participants must submit an abstract of their submission in Microsoft Word format. (maximum 250 words) summarizing the objectives, methods, and findings along with the pre-recorded video. This will be used as a preliminary evaluation criterion.
  • Online Zonal Competitions: Shortlisted participants compete live at zonal levels.
  • Full Paper Submission: Participants selected for Phase 2 are required to submit a full paper (maximum 2,500 words) detailing their research, structured with Title, Abstract, Introduction, Methodology, Results, Discussion, Conclusion, and References.

After the initial two stages of screening, a group of participants will be selected for the following specialized categories:

  • Paper Presentations
  • Poster Presentations
  • Sasthra Jyoti Puraskaram

Participants selected for each category will be notified separately and provided with detailed instructions regarding their respective tracks.

Finalists for Sasthra Jyoti Puraskaram undergo a multi-level evaluation process at Sreedhareeyam, including:

  • Samhitha Parichaya: Familiarity with Ayurvedic classical texts.
  • Vyakhyana Samarthya: Interpretation skills.
  • Karma Kausala: Practical skills in procedures like Kriyakarma.
  • Roga Nirnaya: Diagnostic and problem-solving abilities.
  • Loka Parichaya: General knowledge.
  • Oral Presentation: Articulation of research findings.
Call for Video Presentations and abstract
  • Duration : 8 minutes.

  • The focus of the event is Netra Chikitsa.

    Suggested sub-themes include but not limited to:

    • Disease-specific Studies and Case Reports:
    • Clinical insights into managing eye disorders using Ayurvedic principles.
    • Innovations in integrative approaches for eye diseases.
    • Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) in Ayurveda.
    • Development of SOPs for diagnostics, treatments, and therapeutic interventions.
    • Evidence-based applications of traditional medications for Netra rogas.
    • Diagnostic Advancements in Netra chikitsa:
    • Leveraging modern diagnostic tools in tandem with traditional methods.
    • Kriyakrama (Therapeutic Procedures):
    • Advances in panchakarma and allied therapies in Netra chikitsa.
    • Research Methodologies in Shalakya Tantra: Groundbreaking research methodologies and their application in Ayurveda
  • The content should be well-structured, scientifically accurate, and supported by references from classical texts or contemporary studies.
  • Format : MP4 or AVI.
  • Video Quality : Resolution should be at least 720p, with clear audio. Ensure minimal background noise and stable camera footage to maintain professionalism.
  • Presentation Structure : The video should include the following sections
    • Introduction: Brief introduction of the presenter, affiliation, and an overview of the topic.
    • Objective: Clearly state the objective of the research or insight
    • Methodology: For research submissions, describe the methods used concisely. For clinical insights, outline the treatment procedures.
    • Results/Findings: Summarize key results, observations, or outcomes.
    • Discussion: Discuss the relevance of the findings or insights, highlighting their contribution to the management of Abhishyanda.
    • Conclusion: Summarize key takeaways and potential future directions.
  • Formatting Guidelines
  • Slides
    • Utilize well-designed slides with appropriate visuals such as charts, diagrams, or images to enhance comprehension.
    • Limit the presentation to a maximum of 10 slides
    • Number each slide sequentially for easy reference during the presentation.
  • Audio and Video Quality
    • Ensure the video is recorded in a minimum resolution of 720p.
    • Audio must be clear, with minimal background noise for professional quality.
  • Submission: Email to sunetraconclave@sreedhareeyam.com by December 30, 2024.
Abstract and Full Paper Submission
  • Word Limit: 250 words.
  • Structure: Title, Objectives, Methods, Results, Conclusion.
  • Content Requirements: The abstract should concisely summarize the essence of the research, highlighting the background, rationale, and key findings.
  • Ensure that technical terminology is clearly defined to avoid ambiguity. The content should reflect the originality and relevance of the research to the conclave theme.
  • Formatting: Use Times New Roman, 12 pt, and single spacing. Include all author names, affiliations, and contact details at the top of the abstract.
  • Word Limit: 2,500 words.
  • Format: Times New Roman, 12 pt, line spacing 1.5.
  • Structure
    • Title Page: Include title, author(s) name(s), affiliations, and contact information.
    • Abstract: A brief overview of the study (maximum 250 words).
    • Introduction: Clearly state the background, problem statement, and objectives of the research. Include a review of relevant literature to establish context.
    • Methodology: Provide a detailed account of the materials and methods used, including study design, participant selection, intervention details, data collection techniques, and statistical analyses. Ensure reproducibility by specifying tools, instruments, and dosages.
    • Results: Present the findings in a logical sequence, using tables, figures, and charts where necessary. Include statistical analysis to validate the significance of the results.
    • Discussion: Interpret the results in relation to existing knowledge, addressing the implications of the findings, limitations, and recommendations for future research.
    • Conclusion: Summarize key findings, their relevance to NetraChikitsa management, and any prospective applications.
    • References: Use APA or Vancouver referencing style. Ensure all references are cited accurately within the text.
    • Figures and Tables: Embed relevant visuals within the paper. Provide appropriate captions and ensure all figures and tables are referenced in the text.
  • Submission Requirements: Papers must be submitted in Word and PDF format. Ensure proper alignment, no grammar errors, and consistency in referencing. Use numbered headings for better organization.
  • Dimensions and File Format
    • Aspect Ratio: 16:9 (landscape orientation).
    • Resolution: Minimum 1920 x 1080 pixels for optimal clarity.
    • File Format: PDF only.
  • Content Structure
    • Title
      • Clearly displayed at the top in bold, large font (36–48 pt)
      • Include the title of your study, author(s) names, and affiliations.
    • Introduction
      • Provide background information and state the objective of your study.
    • Methodology
      • Describe the methods used, including experimental setups or treatment protocols.
    • Results
      • Use visuals such as tables, charts, and graphs to present key findings.
    • Discussion
      • Highlight the relevance and implications of your findings
    • Conclusion
      • Summarize the key takeaways
    • References
      • Include up to five references in a standard citation format (e.g., APA, Vancouver).
    • Title: 36–48 pt
    • Subheadings: 24–36 pt
    • Body Text: 18–24 pt
  • Participants will have 5–7 minutes to explain their digital poster to the judges, followed by a 3-minute Q&A session.
